I began riding mountain bikes in my late teens. I grew up in Nebraska so most of my riding was near riparian trails. There was some singletrack, but most of it was through flowing packed dirt trails surrounded by lush, humid, deciduous forests.
College led me out to Boulder, Colorado. During my six years out in Colorado I had the pleasure of exploring a great deal of trails up near the peak to peak highway. Most of the trails consisted of technical singletrack with a lot of switch backs and jutting rocks.
After Colorado I moved to California near Marin County, the birthplace of Mountain Biking. I have taken the opportunity to ride several trails around the area ranging from non-technical double loops like Angel Island to the last legal singletrack in China Camp State Park.

I started out snowboarding when I was 23 after a couple of years skiing. I began after I moved to Colorado. My first real experience on a board was at Breckenridge in Summit County. I quickly grew to love the outdoor sport. I would make it a regular routine to hit the local resort al Eldora near my hometown in Nederland.
After moving out to California I was able to experience the wet snow of the Sierra Nevada Mountain range. I began with a trip to Heavenly resort which had a lot of nice long blue(intermediate) runs. I soon found myself seeking out more technical runs in a mountain called Kirkwood.
